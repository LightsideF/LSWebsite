When to refer

Recognise any of these in a client’s situation?

Accountants, solicitors, and insolvency practitioners refer clients to Lightside when a business debt situation has developed beyond what they can address in their own practice. The referral is usually triggered by one of a handful of recognisable situations — and if any of these are familiar, an initial discussion costs nothing and is completely confidential.

We take over the debt matter entirely. Your client’s relationship with you remains intact and undisturbed. We keep you informed of progress at whatever level of detail is useful, and we work to a conclusion — not just an initial assessment.

A director facing personal exposure from a business failurePersonal guarantees called in, HMRC pursuing the director personally, or a director loan account creating liability — situations where the business position and personal position must be managed together.
Creditor action requiring urgent responseA statutory demand, winding-up petition, or HMRC enforcement where the window for action is short. Early referral consistently produces more options than late referral.
A company closure that needs to be managed carefullyStrike-off, CVL, or MVL — choosing the right route matters for the director’s personal position, especially where personal guarantees or a director loan account are involved.
A client who needs all creditor contact handled for themOnce we are instructed, all creditor contact is redirected to Lightside. Your client stops receiving calls, letters, and debt collection agency contact — immediately.
