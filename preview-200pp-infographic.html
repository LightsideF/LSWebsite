Ms. PA — two PGs, two routes, debt free in twelve months
75% Discount
The Situation

“The business had taken out a Bounce Back Loan hoping revenue would recover. Then additional business borrowing which I personally guaranteed. The business didn’t bounce back. Suddenly I was personally liable for debts I’d never intended to incur, and one creditor moved to make me bankrupt.”

What We Did

We took over both personal guarantees directly and handled them on separate tracks. One creditor had already moved to commence bankruptcy proceedings; we deployed a Breathing Space moratorium to halt that action and negotiated a settlement during the sixty-day window. The second creditor agreed to a monthly repayment plan, which we then positioned for a negotiated settlement — settling at a 75% discount on the amount owed. We also advised against formal liquidation of the company, recommending a managed Strike-Off instead, which avoided £40,000+ in potential personal liabilities and removed the risk of director investigation. Both personal guarantees were resolved within twelve months.

Design notes:

  • Two vertical columns side by side, three steps each. Reads top-to-bottom per track, left-to-right to compare the two PGs.
  • Each column has a header with PG number and a one-line context (italic muted), separated from the steps below by a dashed border.
  • Colour signalling mirrors Mr A’s negotiation timeline on personal-your-property-at-risk.html: coral for adversary action (the creditor moves), teal for Lightside intervention (the mechanism deployed), navy with gold accent for the settled outcome. The semantic is “adversary signal” not “B&D channel signal” — same precedent as Mr A’s case which is on a Personal Debt page.
  • Each step has a small uppercase “who” label (Creditor / Lightside / Settled) at the top, then the action text in Merriweather bold, then optional small meta text underneath for context (60-day moratorium, during the moratorium, on the amount owed).
  • Per Priti’s instruction the “9 months of negotiation” meta text was removed from the PG #2 Lightside step. The text just says “Positioned for negotiated settlement”.
  • The settled outcome boxes (navy with gold accent) tie visually to the case stamp in the header (also gold), creating a narrative loop — the stamp says “75% Discount”, the infographic shows where that came from.
  • Caption underneath summarises the strategic point: tactical range, complete resolution, without bankruptcy/liquidation/losing the home. Italic muted text matching the attribution line below it.
  • Down arrows between steps (↓) show the direction of travel within each track.
  • On mobile (<520px) the two columns stack vertically — PG #1 first, then PG #2. Still legible, just taller.
  • This infographic is a different shape from Mr & Mrs B’s equity comparison (which is a horizontal three-box equation). Different cases warrant different visual structures — Mr & Mrs B’s case is one insight expressible as one equation, Ms PA’s case is two parallel stories.
